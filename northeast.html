Top Beaches — Northeast

This beaches include golden sands, craggy coasts, and beautiful blue waters, making them traveler favorites for anything from relaxing beach days to undersea activities.

1. Ka'anapali Beach

Lahaina, Hawaii

Considered one of the best beaches in America, Ka'anapali is three miles of perfect white sand. Don't miss the daily cliff diving ceremony off Puu Kekaa.“Always beautiful. Beautiful ocean, beach, and plenty of walking and snorkeling room. Always our place to find our peace!”

2. Eagle Beach

Palm - Eagle Beach, Aruba

Eagle Beach, Aruba's biggest beach, is noted for its immaculate and silky white sands, as well as breathtaking Caribbean ocean vistas, adequate parking, shaded places, several beach houses, and a range of water sports.

5. Driftwood Beach

Jekyll Island, Georgia

Stepping into Driftwood Beach at Jekyll Island, Georgia, is like walking into an alternate reality, strewn with the sun-bleached remains of gnarled and worn trees. It feels like what you'd expect a post-apocalyptic world to feel like - evidence of a life that was once intertwined with a life that is now, all under Mother Nature's watching and dominating eye.

7. Hanalei Beach

Hanalei, Hawaii

Hanalei Bay has long been one of Hawaii's most popular tourist destinations, as well as one of the world's most photographed beaches. It has all the perfect ingredients - a wide white sand beach, crystal blue ocean, tropical palm palms, and lush green mountains as a backdrop. At one end of the crescent-shaped bay, the Hanalei River runs into the ocean.

9. Ho'okipa Beach Park

Paia, Hawaii

Ho'okipa has strong waves all year and is popular with advanced surfers and windsurfers. During winter, waves can reach heights of 30 feet and absolutely should only be braved by the most experienced surfers. The beach also has a safe haven for honu (sea turtles), where many gather to sleep at night. Although the beach closes at 7 p.m., you can still spot a couple right before sunset.
