Top Beaches — Northwest

This beaches include golden sands, craggy coasts, and beautiful blue waters, making them traveler favorites for anything from relaxing beach days to undersea activities.

1. Reynisfjara Beach

Vik, Iceland

Reynisfjara is a world-famous black-sand beach found on the South Coast of Iceland, just beside Vik í Myrdal, which is the southernmost village on the mainland of Iceland. With its massive basalt stacks, roaring Atlantic waves, and breathtaking views, Reynisfjara is widely regarded as the most beautiful example of Iceland's black sand beaches.

2. Praia da Falesia

Olhos de Agua, Portugal

The Praia da Falésia is an excellent choice for a day trip to the beach. The beaches are vest, with a wide stretch of golden sands that runs the length of them. The seawater is clean and is only partially exposed to the powerful Atlantic waves that pound the Algarve coastline. These waves may be suitable for surfing in the right conditions.

3.Radhanagar Beach

Havelock Island, Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Radhanagar Beach, located on Havelock Island, is widely regarded as India's best beach. It's a beautiful stretch of sand surrounded by turquoise water and lush forest. Radhanagar Beach, also known as Beach No. 7, appears to be straight out of a postcard. The '7th best beach in the world,' according to Times Magazine.

4. Kelingking Beach

Nusa Penida, Bali

Kelingking Beach is one of Nusa Penida's most well-known attractions, frequently appearing in advertisements for Bali and Indonesia. Its distinctive rock formations resemble a dinosaur's backbone, and it's even been dubbed T-Rex Bay on Google Maps. Visitors may even see Manta rays from the cliff's edge. The popularity of Kelingking Beach has greatly aided Nusa Penida island's tourist growth, particularly through social media. Interestingly, “Kelingking” also means pinky finger in Indonesian, but the reason behind it requires a little imagination.”
